# Pol — Production Bible (real-DAW path)

The browser tools are retired as a production approach. This is the plan to make
**"Pol"** — and the album around it — at the quality of the references, using
real tools, with you directing and me producing.

## The brief (locked)

- **Source:** Googoosh — *Pol* (پل, "the bridge"), *"mano bā khodet bebar"* —
  comp. era ~1976, Cheshmazar-school orchestral pop. Her vocal stays intact and
  forward; it is the protagonist.
- **Reference target:** NU — *Man O To*; Doctor Rockit (Matthew Herbert) —
  *Café de Flore*. Reverent, organic/deep house. Slow (~112–116 BPM). Warm.
- **Album working title:** *Pol* — a bridge between 1976 Tehran and now.
- **Your role:** director. Pick the moments, set the mood, judge takes.
- **My role:** producer. Prep the session, design arrangement and sound,
  drive (or direct a hired engineer through) the DAW work.

## Why the browser tools couldn't get here (so we don't repeat it)

1. They **synthesized** drums/bass from oscillators — can never sound sampled
   and produced like the references.
2. **No time-stretch** — you can't fit a vocal to a new tempo without
   chipmunking pitch. This is the core technical requirement and Web Audio
   can't do it cleanly.
3. Wrong altitude — they demanded musician micro-decisions instead of letting
   you direct.

Real DAWs solve all three: real sample libraries, world-class time-stretch
(Ableton Warp / Logic Flex), and a producer (me) doing the fiddly parts.

## The pipeline

**Stage 0 — Toolchain.** Pick the DAW (see below). Install. One-time setup.

**Stage 1 — Get a clean vocal.** Run *Pol* through stem separation to pull
Googoosh's voice off the 1976 backing (the orchestra/drums are baked into the
master, so we isolate the vocal). Output: a dry-ish vocal stem we can place and
stretch. (This is doable today, free.)

**Stage 2 — Tempo & key map.** Detect the song's original tempo and key. Choose
the project tempo (~114) and a working key. Decide where her phrasing wants to
sit — most likely **half-time** over a 114 four-to-the-floor, the *Man O To* feel.

**Stage 3 — Time-stretch & comp the vocal.** Warp the vocal to project tempo
(pitch preserved). Comp the best phrases: the *"mano bā khodet bebar"* hook, one
verse line, breaths/tails for texture. This is the emotional spine.

**Stage 4 — Build the instrumental from REAL sounds.** Organic-house drum
samples (warm kicks, brushed/shaker percussion, vinyl crackle), a deep sampled
sub/bass, Rhodes/pad chords under her melody, dub delays. Sourced from real
sample libraries or played in. Arranged to the *Man O To* shape:
dust → pulse → her voice enters → groove → breakdown (voice naked) → out.

**Stage 5 — Mix & master.** Balance, glue, space; then a mastering pass (or a
mastering service / engineer) to sit it next to NU on a playlist.

## Toolchain

| Choice | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **DAW** | **Ableton Live** — the organic-house standard; best time-stretch (Warp), best for sampling/looping, huge ecosystem. *Alternative:* **Logic Pro** (Mac-only, ~$200 one-time vs Ableton's higher price, Flex Time is very good) if budget is the deciding factor. |
| **Vocal isolation** | A stem-separation tool to pull her voice from the master. I'll recommend a specific current one when we start Stage 1. |
| **Sounds** | Real organic/deep-house sample packs (drums, textures) + a soft-synth or sampled Rhodes/pads. No browser synthesis. |
| **Mastering** | A mastering service or a freelance engineer for the final polish. |

## Division of labor — two honest models

**A. You drive, I direct (cheapest, real learning curve).** You run the DAW; I
give you exact, click-by-click moves each session and judge the sound with you.
You learn a real skill. Slower, but it's yours.

**B. We hire the hands.** A freelance producer/mix engineer in this genre does
the DAW execution; you + I direct them with the brief, the vocal, and the
references. Fastest to quality; costs money per track. This is the normal
industry model and fits "director + producer."

Either way I do all the prep, arrangement design, and direction. We can also
**start with A for the demo, switch to B for the release master.**
