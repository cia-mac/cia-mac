The chase that isn't there

Twelve lilac dots, one blank gap circling the ring. Watch the gap and you see a chase. Then stare at the centre cross — and the dots turn green, then disappear entirely, leaving a green ghost running through emptiness.

Stage 1 — just watch the moving gap travel around the ring.

Nothing green is ever drawn, and no dot ever leaves. Three constructions stack on the same image. The travelling gap reads as a moving object — your visual system prefers motion to a hole. Fixate and each erased disk leaves a green afterimage in its place, the opponent of lilac. Hold still longer and the unchanging lilac dots fade out of awareness entirely — steady, peripheral, unchanging stimuli are discarded — until only the moving green afterimage remains, chasing around a grey field that contains none of it. Three things you plainly see, none of them present: vision is construction, not recording.
