The window you see through WebGL

Stripes that get finer left-to-right and fainter bottom-to-top, drawn per-pixel on the GPU. The arch where they vanish is not in the image — it is the literal shape of your vision. Lean back; it moves.

Every column has the same stripes; every row the same contrast. A camera's limit would be a flat horizontal line — a fixed threshold. Yours isn't: you see an inverted arch, because mid-range frequencies are where your visual system is most sensitive, and stripes too fine or too coarse vanish even at full contrast. Step back and the whole arch slides right, because spatial frequency is fixed on the screen but changes on your retina with distance. You are not seeing the chart — you are seeing the limits of the instrument that sees it.
