The colour that isn't there

Stare at the cross without moving your eyes. When the disc vanishes, your visual system will paint its opponent colour onto the blank field.

+

Pick a colour, press start, and hold your gaze on the cross. Full screen brightness helps.

20s

While you stared, the cones and opponent channels driven by that colour adapted — their response sagged. Remove the stimulus and the system rebounds past neutral: red–green and blue–yellow are wired as opposing pairs, so fatigue on one side reads as a signal from the other. The colour you just saw on the empty field arrived through no photon at all. It was manufactured entirely by your visual system — vision is construction, not recording.
