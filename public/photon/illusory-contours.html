The triangle that isn't there

Three notched discs. When the notches line up, a solid white triangle appears — with crisp edges and a brightness of its own. Turn them away and it evaporates. Drag the slider.

No triangle is drawn. There is no edge between the "triangle" and the page, and the white inside it is the same white as outside it — measure the pixels and they are identical. Yet you see a bright, sharp-edged shape lying on top of three discs. Given notches that could be explained by an occluding surface, your visual system invents that surface, complete with borders it then perceives as real. It is not filling in a gap in the image — it is constructing an object the image never contained.
