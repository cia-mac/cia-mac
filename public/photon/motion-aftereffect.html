The world slides back

Hold your gaze on the cross while the rings spiral inward. When they stop, the still image will swell toward you — motion with no movement, manufactured by your own visual system.

Pick a direction and press start. Keep your eyes on the centre cross the whole time — don't track the motion.

25s

Direction-selective neurons in area MT fire constantly while you watch one direction of motion, and they adapt — their response sinks. The moment the motion stops, the opposing population, which was never fatigued, now out-votes them, and the brain reads that imbalance as real motion in the opposite direction. A static image visibly moves. This is the waterfall illusion, named by Aristotle and explained only in the last century. The motion you see is not in the image and not on your retina — your visual system is reporting its own exhaustion as a fact about the world.
