Why these belong together

The same dots, every time. Nudge the spacing and the colour, and watch your visual system decide — against your will — what groups with what.

Nothing in the dots says "row" or "column" — that organisation is added by you. Proximity pulls the closer dots into a group; similarity pulls the matching ones. Set them against each other and you can feel the competition: the grid reorganises as one cue overtakes the other. These grouping laws are not learned rules you apply — they are automatic commitments the visual system makes before you get a say. The arrangement you see is authored, not received.
