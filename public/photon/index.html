A series on seeing

Photon to Phenomenology

Interactive pieces built from Stephen Palmer's Vision Science: Photons to Phenomenology. Move a control, watch your own visual system respond. One idea runs through all of them: vision is not recording — it is construction.

  1. 01Three lights, one perceptAdditive colour mixture and the three cone types. §3.2
  2. 02The colour that isn't thereOpponent-process afterimages. This one happens in your eyes, not on the screen. §3.2
  3. 03Two cubes in one drawingNecker-cube multistability, with a measure-your-own-flip-rate experiment. §6.5
  4. 04A cell that watches one spotCentre-surround receptive fields — drag a spotlight, watch the neuron fire. §4.1
  5. 05Why these belong togetherGestalt grouping — set proximity against similarity and feel them compete. §6.1
  6. 06The window you see throughContrast sensitivity — the Campbell–Robson chart, with the arch that is your own vision. §4.2
  7. 07The triangle that isn't thereKanizsa illusory contours — turn the notches and a solid shape appears from nothing. §6.4
